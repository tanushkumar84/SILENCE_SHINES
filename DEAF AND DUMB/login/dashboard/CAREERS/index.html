FAQ's

Is the workplace equipped with accommodations for deaf individuals?

Yes, the workplace is equipped with accommodations such as video relay services, text telephones (TTY), and visual alarms.

What communication methods are used in the workplace?

Communication methods include sign language interpreters, written communication, and video conferencing with captions.

Are there any safety protocols specifically tailored for deaf employees?

Yes, safety protocols include visual alarms, vibrating pagers, and emergency evacuation plans with clear visual instructions.

How will meetings and group discussions be conducted?

Meetings and group discussions will be conducted with accommodations such as sign language interpreters, written agendas, and inclusive communication practices.

Are there any opportunities for professional development related to deaf awareness or accessibility?

Yes, the company provides training sessions and workshops on deaf awareness, communication strategies, and accessibility best practices.

What resources are available for resolving communication barriers with coworkers or supervisors?

Resources include access to sign language interpreters, communication assistance devices, and designated staff members trained in facilitating communication.

How does the company handle discrimination or harassment based on disability?

The company has strict policies against discrimination and harassment based on disability, with procedures for reporting and addressing such incidents promptly.

Are there any deaf employees currently working here who could provide insights or support?

Yes, there are deaf employees who can offer insights, support, and mentorship to new hires or those needing assistance.
